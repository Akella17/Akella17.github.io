About Me

I’m a research intern at TensorLab (Caltech), advised by Dr. Kamyar Azizzadenesheli and Prof. Anima Anandkumar. I also closely collaborate with Prof. Yisong Yue (Caltech), and Dr. Mohammad Ghavamzadeh (Google Research). I am broadly interested in the theory and application of machine learning, with a focus on reinforcement learning, generative modeling, meta-learning, and Bayesian optimization.

Previously, I was a visiting scholar at the AI-NLP-ML lab (IIT Patna), advised by Prof. Asif Ekbal and Prof. Pushpak Bhattacharyya. My work was focused on multimodal fusion and multi-hop question generation. I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee in 2018, where I was fortunate to work with Prof. Vinod Pankajakshan in computer vision and image forensics.

Bayesian Quadrature for Policy Gradient

An introduction to Bayesian quadrature for policy gradient estimation, and the computation methods required for scaling it to high-dimensional settings. Introduces two policy gradient estimators, (i) Deep Bayesian Quadrature Policy Gradient (DBQPG) and (ii) Uncertainty Aware Policy Gradient (UAPG), that serve as a drop-in replacement for Monte-Carlo estimation in most policy gradient algorithms while offering more accurate policy gradient estimates, superior sample complexity, and higher average return.
